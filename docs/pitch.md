# CyberShield Youth UAE — Pitch

## The Problem
UAE youth (ages 15-22) face escalating cyber threats but lack structured, nationally-coordinated cyber hygiene training. Schools have no standardized way to measure or improve student cyber resilience.

## The Solution
**CyberShield Youth UAE** — a national platform that:
1. **Measures** individual cyber resilience through a behavioral assessment (CRI score)
2. **Trains** youth through interactive, category-specific training missions
3. **Monitors** cyber readiness at institutional and national levels

## How It Works
- **Individual:** Youth complete a 12-question assessment across 4 categories (Password Hygiene, Phishing Awareness, Data Privacy, Device Security). They receive a CRI score (0-100) with tier classification and recommendations.
- **Institutional:** Schools see aggregated student scores, category breakdowns, and peer rankings.
- **National:** Government bodies access emirate-level heat maps, tier distributions, and trend analysis.

## Market Opportunity
- 1.2M+ UAE youth in target demographic
- Growing national focus on cybersecurity (UAE Cybersecurity Strategy 2024-2030)
- No existing national youth cyber hygiene platform

## Traction (Demo)
- 12 schools across 7 emirates
- 3,620 students assessed
- National CRI baseline established

## Ask
- Pilot program with 50 schools across 3 emirates
- Integration with national education frameworks
- Endorsement from UAE cybersecurity authorities
